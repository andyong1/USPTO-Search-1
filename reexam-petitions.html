Post-Grant Patent-Owner Petitions

Patent-owner petitions to the Office of Petitions (document code PET.OP) filed after ex parte reexamination was ordered — e.g., requests to reconsider the grant or terminate the proceeding under 35 U.S.C. § 325(d).

About this list. Once a reexamination is ordered (a substantial new question of patentability was found), a patent owner may petition the Office of Petitions to reconsider that grant or terminate the proceeding, often arguing the prior art was already considered under § 325(d). The USPTO codes all such petitions generically as PET.OP ("Petition for review by the Office of Petitions"), so this page lists every post-grant PET.OP and adds a §325(d) badge where the petition's text invokes § 325(d). The § 325(d) flag is detected by reading the petition PDF and is best-effort; verify against the document itself.
